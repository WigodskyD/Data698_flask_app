Data comes from Montgomery County, MD via Data.gov. Accidents and traffic violations within the county are logged with latitude and longitude data to allow comparison. Data was tracked from 2012 to 2019. Montgomery county is located north of the District of Columbia.
Traffic citations included the officers' categorization of each offence. Text mining allowed the large variety of offences to be separated into 23 categories.
A view of one of the datasets: registration and insurance-based violations. Density of the points is difficult to ascertain. Some parts of the county are empty. There is insufficient definition to compare levels directly. There are points geographically coded outside of the county.
To define the edges of the county, queries were sent to the US Census Geocoder API. County identification was separated from the response, which allowed the outline of the county to be defined.
To define points in the county, a radial definition of points was created from a circle with an increasing number points as the radius increased. Points were compared to the county edge to create a full definition to apply a kernel density estimator to.
This is the function used to define our filled-in circle.
Kernel density was fitted for nearby accidents. A cone-shaped smoothing kernel gave the most weight to accidents closest to a point, less to farther accidents. Only locations within the kernel radius were included in each kernel point. The accidents showed a pattern that was balanced between highways and local roads.
The same procedure was used to fit each violation category. Alcohol violations, shown here, were concentrated densely within a few areas.
Violations related to failure to yield to emergency vehicles were concentrated along roadways in the more populous eastern part of the county.
The corner of Wayne Avenue and Georgia Avenue, in Silver Spring, is just outside of Washington, D.C. According to the kernel estimate, it had the highest accident incidence in the county. It is located near a transit center, a shopping area, and many hotels.
This area, on River Rd. in Poolesville, has among the lowest kernel densities for accidents in Montgomery County.
The Montgomery County Agricultural Reserve accounts for 93,000 acres of land in the western and northern part of the county. Within this sparsely populated area, levels of all traffic violations and accidents were much lower than in the more densely populated portions of the county.
